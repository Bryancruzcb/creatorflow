# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Indie game teams, creative studios, technical artists, producers, and release owners who need to understand whether a project can ship with defensible source, license, similarity, and provenance evidence. They work inside large, mixed-format projects and need to investigate findings without uploading private creative assets.

## Product Purpose

CreatorFlow Preflight turns local asset scanning into an accountable release workflow. It indexes a project, surfaces exact and perceptual relationships, lets a human inspect and resolve exceptions, stress-tests complex packages, and exports a portable evidence manifest. Success means a team can explain what was checked, what differed, what permission exists, who made each decision, and what shipped.

## Brand Personality

Classy, exacting, and calm. The product should feel like a high-end studio instrument: technically serious, visually refined, and confident without becoming gamified, theatrical, or branded as an AI product.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI dashboards with purple gradients, glass panels, prompt-first framing, or decorative intelligence scores.
- Gamified security scanners that use noisy badges, achievement language, or exaggerated threat visuals.
- Dense developer consoles that expose implementation detail before the release decision a creative team needs to make.
- Gallery-first products where visual discovery obscures provenance, permission, and release accountability.

## Design Principles

- Show the evidence, not just the score: every similarity claim must expose the actual files, visible differences, method, and limitations.
- Keep creative payloads local by default: remote services receive fingerprints and records, not source files, unless a user explicitly chooses otherwise.
- Make the whole release legible: scanning is one stage in a traceable path from import through human decision to manifest export.
- Earn confidence with honest boundaries: label simulated data, functional browser behavior, and future desktop/server integration separately.
- Preserve creative focus: advanced controls should appear when relevant and never overwhelm the asset being investigated.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All product state must be communicated with text or shape in addition to color. Keyboard focus, touch targets, screen-reader labels, reduced-motion behavior, and responsive layouts are required. Automated blink comparisons must stop or become manual when reduced motion is requested or the document is hidden.
